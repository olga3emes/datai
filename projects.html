Energy

Differential@UPO

Energy efficiency is one of the more relevant objectives for Spanish and European Governments, according to their strategic plans. To appropriately achieve it, an effective monitorization of buildings and transport networks is needed. In the last few years, the development of new sensing technologies, has enabled the successful installation of monitoring devices in different environments and thus, the wide availability of real-time data coming from very diverse elements. However, due to the heterogeneity and diversity of those information flows, coming from very different sensors and sources, it is difficult to appropriate manage, fusing and analysing those data in a integrated way.

This project aims to develop new tools and methods to manage and analyse information coming from several sources with the final goal of better understanding how and when energy is consumed in distributed facilities. In particular, Data Mining and Fuzzy Logic will be mixed together in a context of Big Data to better account for the particularities of energy data coming from several sources.

This is a coordinated project with three complementary research groups from three different universities (Universidad de Granada, Universidad Pablo de Olavide and Universidad de Castilla La Mancha). These universities count with energy monitoring systems that will be employed to tests the results. Additionally, the project counts with the presence of international experts who will collaborate within the different work teams, and several companies in the field of energy have also expressed their interest in the outcomes of the project.

Astronomy

GAIA

We participate in the GAIA project. Gaia is the high accuracy astrometric survey mission of ESA, successfully launched into an L2 orbit on 19 December 2013. Gaia will survey the entire sky down to a magnitude of G=20, detecting about one billion stars, or about 1% of the Galactic stellar population.

The DPAC (Data processing and analysis consortium) is responsible for the data handling from the mission. DPAC is divided into eight subgroups, called coordinating units, each of which is responsible for a different aspect of the Gaia data processing.

Big Data

Big Data & Analysis

This project focus on the development and/or the adaptation of Soft Computing techniques for extracting knowledge from Big Data.

In computer science, soft computing (sometimes referred to as computational intelligence, though CI does not have an agreed definition) is the use of inexact solutions to computationally hard tasks such as the solution of NP-complete problems, for which there is no known algorithm that can compute an exact solution in polynomial time. Soft computing differs from conventional (hard) computing in that, unlike hard computing, it is tolerant of imprecision, uncertainty, partial truth, and approximation. In effect, the role model for soft computing is the human mind.

Soft computing deals with imprecision, uncertainty, partial truth, and approximation to achieve practicability, robustness and low solution cost. As such it forms the basis of a considerable amount of machine learning techniques.

Combining soft computing with Big Data analysis, we develop solutions extracting knowledge of data, and involving evolutionary and swarm intelligence based algorithms and bio-inspired computation.
